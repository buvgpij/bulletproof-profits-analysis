## What Bulletproof Profits Actually Is

Bulletproof Profits is an online video course created under the name Justin Tyler — almost certainly a pen name — that walks you through two Amazon-based business models.

**Amazon FBA**: You buy products cheap (think wholesale from China), ship them to Amazon's warehouses, and Amazon handles storage, packing, shipping, and customer service. You're the seller. You keep the profit margin.

**Amazon Associates (Affiliate Program)**: You don't sell anything. You create content — a blog, YouTube channel, social media page — and recommend Amazon products. When someone clicks your link and buys, you earn a commission, usually between 1% and 10% depending on the category.

Both are legitimate business models. People make real money doing both. The question isn't whether these methods work — they do. The question is whether a $9 course promising $3,750 a day with 7 clicks is the right way to learn them.

**Q: Can beginners really make money with this?**

Yes — but not in 7 clicks or 30 minutes a day. Both Amazon FBA and affiliate marketing are legitimate paths to income, but they require time, effort, and often additional investment beyond the course itself. Beginners can succeed if they're willing to treat this as a real business, not a push-button system.

**Q: What's the difference between Amazon FBA and Amazon Associates?**

Amazon FBA means you're a seller — you buy products, Amazon stores and ships them, and you earn the profit margin after fees. Amazon Associates means you're an affiliate marketer — you promote existing products and earn a commission (1-10%) when someone buys through your link. FBA has higher profit potential but also higher risk and upfront cost. Affiliate marketing has lower risk but takes longer to build meaningful income.

**Q: Is there a refund if I don't like it?**

Yes. The product is sold through Digistore24 and ClickBank, both of which offer a 60-day money-back guarantee. If the training doesn't meet your expectations, you can request a full refund. Be prepared to follow up if the process is slow.
